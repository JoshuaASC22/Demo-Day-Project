Pollution Statistics

Air Pollution

  • Volcanic ash from the eruption of the Indonesian volcano of Krakatoa caused the sky to darken around the world.
  • Due to a pesticide plant accident in Bhopal India, 8,000 people died within days and many more were permanently injured.
  • Mount Vesuvius, a volcano located in Italy, erupted in the year of 1979 and killed hundreds of civilians living in nearby towns of Pompeii and Herculaneum.
  • Most air pollution isn't naturally made, but instead comes from fossil-fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas causing for a colorless and odorless gas called carbon monoxide to be produced.
  • Factories and cars mainly produce common pollutants such as nitrogen oxide, sulfur dioxide, and hydrocarbons.
  • Burning fossil fuels and destroying forests cause a great increase in the amount of fossil fuels in the atmosphere, causing average temperatures around the globe to rise.

Water Pollution

  • Polluted water can either look dirthy with mud, have a nasty smell, and gabage in it or look clean but is filled with ahrmful chemicals that aren't detectable to our 5 senses.
  • Drinking and swining in polluted water is dangerous as it can expose someone to hazardous chemicals that could cause sickness or disease.
  • The United Nations gives and estimation of 4,000 children dying everyday due to drinking polluted water.
  • Polluted water can harm people indirectly through the things that they consume, like fish.
  • Oil and natural gasses are natural causes of polluted water as they leak into oceans and lakes from naturally occuring sources called petroleum seeps.
  • Fertilizer is a major source of water pollution containing nitrogen and phosphorus
  • Chemicals and oils from factories are dumped or seep into the ocean and are called runoff
  • Runoff can cause toxic aquatic environments to form on the ocean and make a great environment for cyanobacteria or blue-green algae
  • Drilling, mining, oil spills, acid mine drainage, and burried chemical waste are all contributes to pollution in the water

Land Pollution

  • Many of the pollutants of the ocean are the same as the pollutants on the land.
  • Plants, animals and people as well can be harmed by pesticides and fertilizers that are blown in the wind.
  • Pesticides can enter the body through consumption.
  • Trash that can is thrown on the ground can increase the difficulty of plants and produces of the food web to create nutrients
  • Inefficient garbage collection can cause for an increase in land pollution.
  • A large landfill close to Quezon City, Philippines was where a great land pollution tragedy took place in 2000 which killed 218 people.
  • Some landfills aren't seal off completely causing some pollutants to contaminate plants and also contaminate the animals that eat it.
  • Kamilo Beach in Hawaii is completely littered in plastic bags and bottles that are brought in through the tide.
